  • UI Design

    • AMA UofM Dearborn Website

      The American Marketing Association at University of Michigan Dearborn challenged me to build a client-accessible site they can post to and show updates from Instagram and a Google Calendar. I chose Wordpress as the base for the post management, then heavily customized every part of the layout. There is custom navigation based on Wordpress categories, an email-sending form I built for new members to join, custom sidebar with extra information, Instagram feed on the top, and a customized Google Calendar widget. The navigation, search, sidebar opening and closing (when mobile or squeezed), and the form are all animated with CSS. Of course, like this site, the entire website dynamically changes based on browser width/device.

      Please check out all of the fancy features at:
      amaumd.com

      Mobile view

      The wordpress theme I worked from already implemented a dynamic layout for all of the various parts of posts, so I modified the styling and hid my sidebar when the browser width was too small. Also changed some font sizes and tweaked other things to be more usable on the mobile web.

  • Graphic Art

    • Timeline Poster

      For this project I had to make a timeline of an artist’s life. I chose Mark Chagall because he used the most color out of the options. I quickly realized 15 images were not going to fit on an 11x17” poster in a straight line, so I developed this idea with the colored lines connecting everything to a timeline on the right. The color scheme, title font, and background shapes are all inspired by his artwork that commonly incorporated multiple colors and geometric shapes.

      A2LP Branding

      During my design process for this brand, I researched multiple other organizations that dealt with language and learning. Many of them utilize a circle-style logo with figures of people. I decided to stick with the circle theme, but make it more relevant. The speech bubbles represent the child’s native and foreign languages. A venn-diagram-style intersection stands for the way the Ann Arbor Languages Partnership helps children bring the two together. Proper spacing, scaling, recoloring, and merchandising are covered in this design guide.
